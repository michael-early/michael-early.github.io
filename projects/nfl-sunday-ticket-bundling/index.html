NFL Sunday Ticket: Is It Profit Maximizing?

Industrial Organization · Theory · Bundling

Core Question

Why does the NFL sell Sunday Ticket exclusively as a full bundle of all teams rather than offering team-based or à la carte pricing, even when the bundle appears inefficient to consumers?

Model Framework

  • Monopoly with antitrust exemption
  • Consumers with heterogeneous willingness to pay
  • Pure bundling vs mixed bundling strategies

Key Economic Insight

The NFL maximizes profit by forcing pure bundling because most consumers have high willingness to pay for only one team, but relatively low willingness to pay for others. Bundling allows the league to extract more producer surplus than team-based pricing would allow.

Theoretical Foundations

  • Adams & Yellen (1976): Commodity Bundling
  • Price discrimination under monopoly
  • Negative correlation of valuations across consumers

Implications

  • Explains persistence of high Sunday Ticket prices
  • Predicts similar bundling in markets with substitution across goods
  • Clarifies why team-based packages are unlikely under current incentives
