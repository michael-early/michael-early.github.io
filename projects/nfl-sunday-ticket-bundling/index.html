NFL Sunday Ticket Pricing Model

Industrial organization · Theory · Sports economics

Problem

The NFL sells Sunday Ticket only as a full bundle of all teams, not team-based or à la carte, even though many consumers want just one team. I asked: is this pure bundling profit-maximizing, and why would the league choose it over alternatives?

Method

This is a theory-only project—no empirical data. I built a model of a monopoly (NFL with antitrust exemption) with consumers who have heterogeneous willingness to pay across teams, and compared pure bundling to mixed bundling. The analysis draws on Adams & Yellen (1976) and price discrimination under negative correlation of valuations across consumers.

Result

Pure bundling is profit-maximizing here because most consumers value one team highly and others little; bundling lets the league extract more surplus than team-based pricing. That explains high Sunday Ticket prices and why team-only packages are unlikely under current incentives. The full model and implications are in the paper below.
