NFL Quarterback Wage Discrimination

Labor economics · Sports analytics · Regression analysis

Problem

Do NFL quarterbacks receive different guaranteed compensation on their second contracts by race after controlling for performance and market conditions? Answering this tests whether labor markets in a high-visibility, performance-tracked setting show wage gaps unexplained by productivity.

Method

I built a dataset of quarterbacks drafted 2011–2020, with guaranteed money on second contracts (Spotrac), QBR, draft position, and team salary cap. I ran OLS regressions with a race indicator and controls for performance, draft capital, and cap space, and used a RESET test to check functional form.

Result

After controlling for on-field performance and team context, quarterback salary differences were explained by measurable productivity rather than race. Pay was most strongly driven by QBR and available team cap space, indicating teams price quarterbacks as performance-dependent assets rather than demographic signals.
