About Me

I am a Master’s student in Economics specializing in quantitative methods, causal inference, and applied data science. My work applies econometric identification strategies — difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, instrumental variables — alongside machine learning for prediction, classification, and NLP.

I focus on labor markets, policy evaluation, and market structure, building analyses from raw data through modeling to interpretable results. I emphasize rigorous empirical design and clean, reproducible code.
